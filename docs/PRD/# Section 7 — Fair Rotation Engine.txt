# Section 7 — Fair Rotation Engine & Auto-Plan Generation

## Purpose

The Fair Rotation Engine is responsible for helping leaders distribute assignments fairly while maintaining operational flexibility.

The goal is not to replace leadership decisions.

The goal is to:

* Reduce repetitive assignments
* Improve labor distribution
* Speed up planning
* Provide recommendations
* Generate fair plans automatically

Leaders always maintain final authority.

---

# Planning Philosophy

The system should assist.

The system should not force.

Warnings may be ignored by authorized leaders.

Examples:

* John worked CL yesterday
* Maria unloaded yesterday
* Carlos worked Put Away yesterday

The system may recommend alternatives.

The leader can still assign them if operationally necessary.

---
## Rotation-First Planning Philosophy

ShiftFlow shall prioritize assignment rotation whenever operationally possible.

The objective is to ensure associates perform different planned assignments across consecutive workdays.

Example:

Monday:
John → CL

Tuesday:
John → Loader

Wednesday:
John → FLR

Thursday:
John → Tile Overbox

The system should attempt to distribute assignments fairly across all associates.

The Fair Rotation Engine should evaluate:

* Previous planned assignments
* Assignment frequency
* Recent assignment history
* Team-wide task distribution
* Equipment certifications
* Operational requirements

The goal is not only to avoid repeating yesterday's task.

The goal is to create balanced assignment exposure across multiple tasks over time.

The engine should prefer assignments that an associate has performed less frequently.

Leaders may override recommendations when operational requirements demand it.

Rotation calculations shall use Planned Assignment History rather than Activity History.


# Planned Assignment History Rule

The Fair Rotation Engine shall use:

Planned Assignment History

as its primary source of rotation decisions.

The engine shall not primarily use:

Activity History

for fairness calculations.

---

# Example

Monday

Planned Assignment:

John → CL

Activities:

John → CL
John → Loader
John → Housekeeping

Tuesday

The Fair Rotation Engine should consider:

John → CL yesterday

not:

John → Loader yesterday

because Loader was only a temporary operational reassignment.

This rule applies to:

* Inbound
* Outbound
* Training assignments

* ICQA assignments

# Auto-Plan Inputs

The Auto-Plan Generator should evaluate:

* Department
* Shift Key
* Date
* Available associates
* Call-offs
* Overtime
* Equipment certifications
* Active dock doors
* Templates
* Planned Assignment History
* Rotation rules
* Special assignments
* Training assignments

---

# Auto-Plan Priority Order

The planning engine should prioritize:

1. Associate availability
2. Equipment certification requirements
3. Department compatibility
4. Shift key compatibility
5. Fair rotation recommendations
6. Historical planning patterns
7. Template requirements

---

# Equipment Validation

The engine shall never assign an associate to equipment they are not certified to operate.

Example:

Task:
CL

Requires:
Clamp

If John is not Clamp certified:

John cannot be automatically assigned.

---

# Rotation Warnings

Examples:

Outbound:

"John was planned for CL yesterday. Are you sure?"

Inbound:

"Maria was planned for Unload yesterday. Are you sure?"

The leader may:

* Accept recommendation
* Override recommendation

---

# Rotation Lookback Period

The system should support configurable lookback periods.

Examples:

* Yesterday
* Last 3 days
* Last week
* Last 30 days

Default recommendation:

Previous day.

---

# Assignment Frequency Tracking

The system should track how often associates perform:

* CL
* FLR
* OBL
* Unload
* Put Away
* Middle Mile
* Support Outbound
* Housekeeping
* ICQA

This data supports future analytics.

---

# Auto-Plan Confidence

The generated plan should provide a confidence score.

Example:

95%
Excellent rotation and certification coverage.

82%
Minor rotation conflicts detected.

65%
Several rotation conflicts detected.

This helps leaders quickly evaluate plan quality.

---

# Special Assignment Protection

Associates assigned to:

* Training
* ICQA
* Middle Mile
* Support Outbound
* Overtime

should be considered occupied.

The Auto-Plan Generator should not double-assign them.

Future versions may include:

The architecture should support future AI-based planning features.

---

# Operational Philosophy

The Fair Rotation Engine exists to assist leaders.

The system should:

* Recommend
* Warn
* Suggest

but never remove leader control.

Human leadership decisions always take priority over automation.

## Leadership Override Principle

ShiftFlow shall recommend fair assignment rotation whenever operationally possible.

However, leadership decisions always take precedence.

The system may:

* Recommend
* Suggest
* Warn
* Score

The system shall not:

* Prevent assignments
* Block plan publication
* Force rotation

Examples:

* John worked CL yesterday.
* Maria unloaded yesterday.
* Carlos worked Put Away twice this week.

The system may present a rotation notice.

Leaders may always choose to proceed with the assignment.

ShiftFlow exists to assist leadership decisions, not replace them.

Human operational judgment remains the final authority.
