# Section 6 — Inbound Planning Workflow

## Purpose

The Inbound Planning Workflow is responsible for managing:

* Trailer unloading
* Dock door assignments
* Put Away operations
* Middle Mile operations
* Support Outbound operations
* Housekeeping assignments
* Training assignments
* Labor movement throughout the day

Unlike Outbound, which is primarily task-based, Inbound is operational-task driven and heavily influenced by dock door activity.

---

# Inbound Planning Philosophy

The system should not ask leaders to build plans from scratch every day.

The application already knows:

* Inbound associates
* Shift Key
* Available equipment
* Dock door list
* Historical assignments
* Templates

The leader should only provide daily exceptions.

---

# Step 1 — Login

Leader logs into ShiftFlow.

Example:

John
Inbound Leader

The system verifies:

* Authentication
* Role
* Department permissions

---

# Step 2 — Select Key

Leader selects:

* Key 1
* Key 2
* Key 3

The system loads:

* Inbound associates
* Dock doors
* Templates
* Rotation history
* Equipment
* Historical planning data

for the selected key.

---

# Step 3 — Morning Setup Wizard

Inbound only asks for exceptions.

---

## Call-Offs

Question:

Any call-offs today?

Leader selects associates.

Call-off associates are removed from planning calculations.

---

## Overtime

Question:

Any overtime support today?

If yes:

Leader selects associates and their assignments.

Example:

Maria
Task: Put Away
Equipment: Pacer

---

## Middle Mile Ownership

Question:

Will Inbound handle Middle Mile today?

Options:

* Yes
* No

If Yes:

Leader assigns associates.

If No:

Inbound board displays:

Handled by Outbound

---

## Support Outbound

Question:

Will Inbound support Outbound today?

If Yes:

Leader assigns associates.

Support associates become unavailable for normal inbound assignments.

---

## Training

Question:

Any training assignments today?

If Yes:

Leader creates:

* Trainer
* New Hire
* Task
* Equipment

Training pairs are treated as active assignments.

---

# Step 4 — Dock Door Selection

The dock door list is configurable.

Examples:

* Door 635
* Door 622
* Door 618
* Door 640

The system loads all available doors.

The leader selects which doors are active today.

Example:

Active Doors:

* 635
* 622
* 618

Only active doors participate in plan generation.

---

# Step 5 — Auto-Generate Plan

The system generates assignments using:

* Available associates
* Key
* Active dock doors
* Equipment certifications
* Planned assignment history
* Fair rotation rules
* Overtime
* Training
* Support assignments
* Call-offs

---

# Step 6 — Review Generated Plan

The generated plan should display operational assignments.

Examples:

Door 635
John
Unload
Clamp

Door 622
Maria
Unload
Pacer

Put Away
Carlos

Middle Mile
Alex

Support Outbound
Ana

Each assignment card includes:

* Edit
* Switch
* Remove

---

# Step 7 — Switch Feature

The leader may switch associates.

Example:

John
Unload Door 635

Maria
Put Away

Leader selects:

Switch

Result:

John → Put Away

Maria → Unload Door 635

System validates:

* Certifications
* Rotation rules
* Equipment requirements

before applying the switch.

---

# Step 8 — Publish Plan

Leader publishes the plan.

The plan becomes visible in:

* Dashboard
* TV Mode
* Presentation Mode

Published plans become the official Planned Assignment History.

---

# Step 9 — Live Operations

Inbound operations change frequently.

The system must support real-time labor movement.

Examples:

* Trailer finishes
* New trailer arrives
* Labor support needed
* Housekeeping starts
* Outbound needs support

Leaders must be able to:

* Move associates
* Switch associates
* Reassign associates
* Create new assignments

without rebuilding the plan.

---

# Available Pool

A dedicated Available Pool must exist.

Available Pool contains:

Associates who:

* Are present
* Are not assigned
* Are not on break
* Are not on lunch
* Are not inactive

The leader can quickly assign available associates.

Example:

Available:

* John
* Carlos
* Maria

Assign To:

* Door 640
* Put Away
* Middle Mile
* Support Outbound
* Housekeeping

---

# Trailer Completion Flow

When a trailer finishes:

Leader selects:

Complete

The associate automatically moves to:

Available Pool

Example:

Door 635 completed

John becomes Available

Leader can immediately assign John to:

* Another Door
* Put Away
* Middle Mile
* Support Outbound
* Housekeeping

---

# Activity Tracking

All labor movement throughout the day must be stored.

Example:

John

08:00 AM
Unload Door 635

10:30 AM
Put Away

01:00 PM
Middle Mile

03:30 PM
Support Outbound

This data becomes part of Activity History.

---

# Shift Close

At the end of the shift:

Leader closes the plan.

The system stores:

* Planned Assignment History
* Activity History
* Assignment timeline
* Overtime records
* Training records
* Support records
* Middle Mile records

The shift becomes available for reporting and future fairness calculations.

---

# Operational Principle

Inbound planning must prioritize:

* Speed
* Flexibility
* Reassignment capability
* Visibility
* Dock door awareness
* Real-time updates

The leader should never need to rebuild the entire plan because a trailer finished or labor needs changed.
