# Section 4.1 — Planned Assignment History vs Activity History

## Overview

ShiftFlow shall maintain two separate historical records:

### Planned Assignment History

Represents the original assignment generated or approved by the leader during plan creation.

Examples:

Monday:

* John → CL
* Maria → FLR
* Carlos → Zone 1 Loader

Tuesday:

* John → FLR
* Maria → OBL
* Carlos → CL

This history represents leadership planning decisions.

---

### Activity History

Represents what actually occurred throughout the day.

Examples:

Monday:

8:00 AM
John → CL

11:30 AM
John → Zone 1 Loader

2:00 PM
John → Housekeeping

4:00 PM
John → Support ICQA

All operational changes must be recorded.

---

## Fair Rotation Engine Rules

The Fair Rotation Engine must primarily use:

* Planned Assignment History

The Fair Rotation Engine should NOT use temporary reassignment history as the primary source for rotation decisions.

Example:

Monday:
John planned assignment = CL

During the day:
John moved to Loader

Tuesday:
The system should consider:

"John worked CL yesterday."

not:

"John worked Loader yesterday."

because Loader was only a temporary operational reassignment.

---

## Reporting

Leaders should be able to view:

### Planned History

Used for:

* Rotation analysis
* Workforce balancing
* Planning trends

### Activity History

Used for:

* Operational analysis
* Labor movement tracking
* Productivity analysis
* Historical audits

---

## Assignment Timeline

Every associate shall have a timeline view.

Example:

John

08:00 AM → CL
11:30 AM → Zone 1 Loader
02:00 PM → Housekeeping
04:00 PM → ICQA Support

This timeline must be preserved permanently unless company retention rules require archiving.

---

## Operational Philosophy

Plans represent leadership intent.

Activities represent operational reality.

ShiftFlow must preserve both.
