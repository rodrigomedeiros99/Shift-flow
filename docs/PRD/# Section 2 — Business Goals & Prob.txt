# Section 2 — Business Goals & Problems Being Solved

## Current State

Warehouse labor planning is currently performed using a combination of:

* Excel spreadsheets
* Screenshots displayed on TVs
* Whiteboards
* Verbal communication
* Manual reassignment throughout the day

While these methods work, they create operational inefficiencies, lack historical visibility, and require significant manual effort from leaders.

ShiftFlow exists to modernize and simplify these processes.

---

# Problem 1 — Daily Planning Takes Too Long

## Current Process

Every morning leaders manually:

* Build labor plans
* Assign associates
* Consider overtime
* Consider call-offs
* Consider training
* Consider support assignments
* Consider equipment availability
* Update spreadsheets

The process is repetitive and time-consuming.

## Goal

Allow leaders to generate a daily plan within minutes using:

* Templates
* Auto-plan generation
* Fair rotation logic
* Associate history
* Equipment validation

---

# Problem 2 — Plans Become Outdated Immediately

## Current Process

A leader creates an Excel sheet.

A screenshot is placed on a TV.

Throughout the day:

* People move
* Tasks change
* Support assignments happen
* Trailers finish
* Housekeeping starts

The TV no longer reflects reality.

## Goal

Provide a live labor board that updates in real time.

All changes should instantly appear on:

* Dashboard
* TV mode
* Presentation mode

---

# Problem 3 — Whiteboard Dependency

## Current Process

Leaders often maintain a physical whiteboard showing:

* Associate names
* Current assignments
* Reassignments

This information exists only on the board and is lost later.

## Goal

Replace the whiteboard with a digital operational board.

Provide:

* Live updates
* Searchability
* History
* Reporting

---

# Problem 4 — Assignment Fairness

## Current Process

Leaders attempt to rotate associates fairly.

However:

* Previous assignments are difficult to track
* Leaders may unintentionally repeat assignments
* Associates may perform the same tasks repeatedly

## Goal

Create a Fair Rotation Engine.

The system should:

* Track assignment history
* Warn leaders when assignments repeat too often
* Suggest fair alternatives
* Support automatic fair planning

Example:

"John picked CL yesterday. Are you sure you want to assign him to CL again today?"

---

# Problem 5 — Lack of Historical Visibility

## Current Process

There is no easy way to answer:

* What did John do last week?
* How often has Maria unloaded trailers?
* Who handled Middle Mile recently?
* Who has been assigned to FLR repeatedly?

## Goal

Maintain complete assignment history.

Allow leaders to filter by:

* Associate
* Department
* Key
* Date range
* Task
* Equipment
* Area
* Dock door

---

# Problem 6 — Overtime Planning

## Current Process

Leaders manually remember:

* Who is staying over
* What task they will perform
* Which department they support

## Goal

Support overtime planning during morning setup.

Allow leaders to:

* Add overtime associates
* Assign tasks
* Assign equipment
* Include overtime in auto-plan generation

---

# Problem 7 — Call-Off Management

## Current Process

Call-offs require manual adjustments.

Leaders must rebuild portions of the plan.

## Goal

Allow quick call-off entry.

The system should automatically:

* Remove unavailable associates
* Recalculate assignments
* Suggest replacements

---

# Problem 8 — Inbound and Outbound Planning Differences

## Current Process

Inbound and Outbound have fundamentally different workflows.

Inbound:

* Dock door focused
* Trailer unloading
* Put Away
* Middle Mile
* Support Outbound

Outbound:

* Picking
* Loading
* Zone assignments
* Overbox operations

## Goal

Provide separate planning experiences optimized for each department.

Inbound should be door-driven.

Outbound should be task/area-driven.

---

# Problem 9 — Training Visibility

## Current Process

Training assignments are often tracked separately.

Leaders must remember:

* Trainer
* New hire
* Assigned area
* Equipment

## Goal

Support training assignments directly inside ShiftFlow.

The system should treat training pairs as operational assignments.

---

# Problem 10 — TV Meeting Visibility

## Current Process

Morning meetings require leaders to explain plans using spreadsheets and screenshots.

Inbound and Outbound leaders speak separately.

## Goal

Provide a Presentation Mode.

Support:

* Outbound view
* Inbound view
* Full view
* Live TV updates

Leaders should be able to toggle presentations during meetings.

---

# Problem 11 — Operational Flexibility

Warehouse operations change constantly.

Examples:

* Trailer finishes early
* Labor needs shift
* Housekeeping begins
* ICQA needs support
* Outbound needs support
* Middle Mile changes ownership

## Goal

Provide rapid reassignment capabilities.

Support:

* Move
* Switch
* Edit
* Remove
* Reassign

without rebuilding the entire plan.

---

# Business Goal Summary

ShiftFlow will:

* Reduce planning time
* Increase visibility
* Improve fairness
* Improve communication
* Reduce manual effort
* Improve historical tracking
* Improve labor allocation decisions
* Replace spreadsheets and whiteboards
* Create a scalable warehouse planning platform
